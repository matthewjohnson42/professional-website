Introduction

I graduated cum laude in December 2017 from Augsburg University with a Bachelor of the Sciences degree in Computer Science and a Bachelor of the Arts degree in Computational Philosophy. I chose to study these subjects as I have a long standing interest in the logical structures that give form to the world of the individual and that are revealed to us through a precise inspection of the intersubjective world of the social organism. Computer Science was a practical application of this curiosity and Computational Philosophy, if nothing more, was a wonderful excuse to be able to study the foundations of reason and meaning as they are represented in the traditions of western philosophy. I also took a minor in Mathematics while studying at Augsburg University.

Prior to my studies at Augsburg University in Minneapolis, Minnesota, I had worked at United HealthGroup where I performed the duties of a software quality assurance consultant. These duties involved developing an understanding of the function of the project I was assigned to and using that understanding to ensure that such a function was realized with minimal errors. Before that, I had also spent time studying at Century College in White Bear Lake, Minnesota. It was there that I determined that the study of Computer Science would be a profitable course of action that I would be comfortable with pursuing.

Currently I am looking for employment as a software developer. I would like to specialize as a Java developer and later move on towards doing development in big data. My motivation for a pursuit of a career as a Java developer in big data is to be able to work with large volumes of textual data doing analysis of that data. This would be the realm of things like Salesforce Einstein, email spam filters, Google search query processing and prediction, or the auto-complete feature included with many modern cell phones. I find language to be a very curious thing as it seems to create a sort of boundary for human knowledge, one that limits knowledge to that which is articulable, expressable, or actionable. Everything else is a sort of holiday language, something that we must pass over in silence. It is this curiosity of constraint that pulls me forward.

Personal

I like a lot of stuff. Almost everything. At some point in the past, I had a realization that I would be unable to literally know everything and do anything. It would appear for myself there simply is not enough time to know all Mathematics, all Physics, all Chemistry, all of Science, all of Philosophy, all of that which gives meaning to humanity; that there is not enough time to see every point on the planet and to have conversations with every one of its inhabitants, that I lack the time to be able to experience all that this planet has to offer. As a result of this realization, I am now doing my best to stay focused on several areas in which I might develop proficiency and demonstrate excellence while simultaneously retaining a curiosity that is inclined to consider the broader and wider fullness that is the human experience. So far it seems like the areas that I have the most drive for are computer science and linguistics, along with camping to keep me anchored in the real world. I am not always convinced that these interests are anything but arbitrary, and there are days where this drive is greater than others. Despite this, I continue along with the subjects that I have thus far chosen to pursue.
